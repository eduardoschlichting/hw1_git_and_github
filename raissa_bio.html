About my name

My parents, Rose and Roberto, decided to have 3 children named with the same first letter. One day, in a magazine, they saw a name in an article. They named me after Raisa Gorbachyova (in portuguese her first name is written as Raissa). She was the wife of soviet leader Mikhail Gorbachev. but my parents just wanted a name with R and liked hers, there’s no tribute intended.

In my birth certificate it's written Raíssa, but that's the only document that uses this accentuation so I don't bother much on how people write this part. In the brazilian pronunciation, when R begins a word, it sounds like H like in how, here, heat, home. So, originally, my name begins with that strong sound, but I really don't mind, if it's easier for you, you can call me Shakira - wherever, whenever.

Figueiredo isn't a middle name. It's not unusual in Brazil to name someone with a surname from the father side and other from the mother side. So here I am, Raissa Figueiredo Prado, usually just Raissa Prado. But if you read until here, you can call me Raissa or Ra.
